sort1 uses: bubble sort

How do you know?: it gives better results for already sorted lists than the sort3, because if the list is sorted there will be no swaps (lower bound is O(n))

sort2 uses: merge sort

How do you know?: it performs better for all types of lists than the other two.

sort3 uses: selection sort

How do you know?: It gives the worst resuls for already sorted lists, because its lower bound is O(n2).
